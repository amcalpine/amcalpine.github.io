Alys McAlpine

I am from Washington, D.C. — a way less square city than the map suggests!

I relocated to London in 2013, by way of Boston for my undergraduate degree, to complete a Masters at The London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine (LSHTM). During this time, I was fortunate to find an academic home at the LSHTM Gender Violence and Health Centre (GVHC) working with colleageues on applied research to inform prevention of the most prevalent and pervasive forms of violence, exploitation, and discrimination. In 2015, I joined the GVHC as a staff researcher and then in 2017 transitioned back to full time student to pursue my PhD. My current research is at the intersection of violence, labour exploitation, migration, gender, public health and complex systems.

I am supervised by Professor Cathy Zimmernam and Dr Ligia Kiss. I am also advised by Honorary Professor Zaid Chalabi on the methods of Agent-Based Modelling. My PhD is being completed in collaboration with The Freedom Fund and funded by the ESRC UBEL DTP. In 2021, I completed the final stages of my PhD as part of the Alan Turing Institute's PhD Enrichment Scheme.
