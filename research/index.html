Research

Current PhD Research

I am enrolled in a PhD program in Public Health and Policy at LSHTM. My PhD thesis explores low-wage labour migration pathways between Myanmar and Thailand to inform safer migration interventions with the aim of preventing human trafficking and labour exploitation.

This research incorporates complex systems and social network theory using egocentric network analysis (Figure 1) and multi-scale agent-based modelling (Figure 2 and Interactive ABM below). This model aims to explore how migrants, social and intermediary networks, and employers form a complex system and to understand patterns that emerge.
